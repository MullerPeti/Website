# Admirals (Admiral Markets)

**Website:** https://admiralmarkets.com/  
## Short summary

Admirals is a long-established, EU-regulated broker offering MT4 and MT5 with a broad CFD product range.  
It is primarily positioned toward European retail traders who value strong regulation and a wide instrument selection over ultra-low trading costs.

## Broker overview

Admirals operates multiple regulated entities within the European Union and the UK, making it one of the more conservatively structured retail brokers in the MT4/MT5 ecosystem.  
Client onboarding, leverage limits, and product availability depend on the specific entity and jurisdiction.

The broker focuses on transparency and regulatory compliance rather than aggressive pricing or promotional features.

## Trading platforms

- MetaTrader 4  
- MetaTrader 5  
- Web-based trading interface

Both MT4 and MT5 are supported with standard functionality, including expert advisors (EAs), indicators, and custom scripts.  
Execution quality is generally stable, though spreads are not among the tightest in the industry.

## Regulation and legal structure

Admirals operates under several well-known regulators:

- FCA (United Kingdom)  
- CySEC (Cyprus)  
- EFSA (Estonia)

EU client accounts fall under ESMA rules, including leverage caps, negative balance protection, and standardized risk disclosures.

US residents are not accepted.

## Costs and execution

Trading costs are typically **medium** compared to other MT-focused brokers:

- Spreads are generally wider than raw/ECN-style brokers
- Commission-free pricing is common on retail accounts
- Execution is stable but not optimized for ultra-high-frequency or latency-sensitive strategies

Admirals is therefore less suited for extreme scalping compared to low-latency ECN brokers.

## Suitable use cases

Admirals may be appropriate for:

- Swing trading on FX and indices  
- Longer-horizon systematic strategies  
- Traders prioritizing EU regulation and legal clarity  
- Manual trading and lower-frequency EAs  

It is generally **not optimal** for:

- High-frequency scalping  
- Cost-sensitive grid or martingale strategies  
- Traders seeking offshore leverage conditions
