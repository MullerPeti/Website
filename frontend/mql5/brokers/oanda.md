# OANDA

**Website:** https://www.oanda.com/

## Short summary

OANDA is a long-established, highly regulated forex broker known for pricing transparency and a conservative, compliance-focused operating model.

## Broker overview

OANDA operates under multiple top-tier regulators and is one of the few retail forex brokers that accepts both EU and US clients.  
Its offering is structured around transparency, standardized pricing, and regulatory robustness rather than aggressive leverage or promotional features.

The broker is commonly used by discretionary traders and institutions requiring clear pricing models and strong legal protections.

## Regulation and legal structure

OANDA operates under several major regulatory authorities:

- Commodity Futures Trading Commission / National Futures Association (United States)  
- Financial Conduct Authority (United Kingdom)  
- Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)  
- Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)  

US and EU residents are accepted under their respective regulated entities.  
Leverage limits, margin requirements, and product availability vary significantly by jurisdiction.

## Costs and execution

Trading costs at OANDA are typically **medium**:

- No minimum trade size on many instruments  
- Transparent, spread-only pricing model on most accounts  
- Spreads are generally wider than ECN-style brokers  

Execution is stable and reliable but not optimized for high-frequency or latency-sensitive strategies.

## Suitable use cases

OANDA may be appropriate for:

- Swing trading and discretionary trading  
- Traders prioritizing regulatory protection and transparency  
- Trading forex and selected commodity CFDs  
- Educational and research-oriented trading workflows  

It is generally less suitable for:

- Cost-sensitive scalping strategies  
- High-frequency algorithmic trading  
- Traders seeking aggressive leverage conditions  

## User base and reputation

OANDA reports a global active user base in the range of **50k+ clients**, reflecting its focus on regulated markets rather than mass retail expansion.  
Public feedback typically highlights transparency and stability, with mixed opinions on spreads and platform flexibility.

As with all brokers, trading conditions depend on jurisdiction, regulatory entity, and account configuration.
