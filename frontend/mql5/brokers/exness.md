# Exness

**Website:** https://www.exness.com/

## Short summary

Exness is a high-volume forex and CFD broker primarily known for tight spreads, high liquidity, and broad support for MetaTrader-based automated trading.

## Broker overview

Exness operates multiple regulated entities across different jurisdictions, serving a large international retail client base.  
The broker is widely used in algorithmic and short-term trading communities due to its pricing model, execution characteristics, and flexible account structures.

Client onboarding, leverage, and available instruments depend strongly on the specific legal entity and region.

## Trading platforms

- MetaTrader 4  
- MetaTrader 5  
- Web-based trading interface  

Both MT4 and MT5 are fully supported, including expert advisors, indicators, and custom scripts.  
Exness is commonly used for automated strategies, including cost-sensitive systems, due to generally competitive spreads and execution.

## Regulation and legal structure

Exness operates under several regulatory authorities:

- Financial Conduct Authority (United Kingdom)  
- Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission (CySEC)  
- Financial Sector Conduct Authority (South Africa)  
- Financial Services Authority (Seychelles)  

Despite EU-regulated entities existing within the group, retail onboarding for EU residents may be restricted depending on the current regulatory setup.  
US residents are not accepted.

## Costs and execution

Trading costs at Exness are typically **low**, particularly on commission-based account types:

- Tight spreads on major FX pairs  
- Competitive commission structures on raw accounts  
- Execution suitable for most retail algorithmic and short-term strategies  

Conditions can vary meaningfully between account types and regulatory entities.

## Suitable use cases

Exness may be appropriate for:

- Automated trading using MT4 or MT5  
- Scalping and short-term strategies  
- Cost-sensitive systems and grid-based approaches  
- High-frequency retail trading within broker-imposed limits  

It may be less suitable for:

- Traders requiring strict EU-only regulatory protection  
- Long-term investing-oriented workflows  

## User base and reputation

Exness reports a global user base in the range of **700k+ active clients**.  
Public feedback often emphasizes pricing and execution quality, while regulatory structure and regional availability are common discussion points.

As with all brokers, actual trading conditions depend on jurisdiction, entity, and account configuration.
